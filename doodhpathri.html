Doodhpathri

Back to Home

Kashmir's Quiet Secret

Most Kashmir visitors never make it to Doodhpathri. The tour buses go to Gulmarg, Pahalgam, and Sonamarg. Doodhpathri does not have a ski resort or a famous glacier. What it has is something increasingly rare in popular tourist regions: genuine quiet. The name means “Valley of Milk” and comes from the Shaliganga River that runs through it, milky white from glacial rock flour. The valley sits at 2,730 metres in the Budgam district, about 42 km from Srinagar.

The Meadow and What Surrounds It

The central draw is the meadow: a wide, flat, grassy expanse surrounded by dense deodar and pine forest, with the river running along one edge and mountains visible in every direction. In May and June wildflowers cover the ground in purples, yellows, and whites. In summer the grass is a vivid green. This is the kind of place where you can sit for an hour and hear nothing except wind, water, and birds.

Before reaching the main meadow, the road passes through Tangnar, a small settlement good for photography. Further along, Mujhpathri is a hamlet with traditional Gujjar shepherd houses. Palmaidan, just beyond the main meadow, is a rougher landscape of large boulders scattered across a plateau with streams running between them.

Practical Notes

Doodhpathri is not set up for mass tourism. There are a few guesthouses and a JKTDC property, but no large hotels, no reliable ATMs, and limited food options. Bring cash, carry water and snacks. For most visitors this is part of the appeal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you get to Doodhpathri from Srinagar?

By car it is about 42 km, taking roughly 1.5 hours. You can hire a car from Srinagar for a Doodhpathri day trip, or include it in a longer Kashmir Valley package.

What is the best time to visit Doodhpathri?

May to September. Wildflowers are most impressive in June and early July. Winter access is limited as the valley can be completely snow-covered from November to April.

Can you visit Doodhpathri and Gulmarg on the same day?

They are in opposite directions from Srinagar, so combining them in one day would be very rushed. It is better to dedicate a separate day to each.
