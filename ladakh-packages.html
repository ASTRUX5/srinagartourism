About Ladakh

Ladakh sits at an average altitude of 3,500 metres and rising. The thin air means acclimatisation is essential — we include a rest day in all packages. The landscape is extraordinary: high-altitude deserts, turquoise lakes, Buddhist monasteries perched on cliffs, and passes that cross over 5,000 metres.

The best time to visit Ladakh is June to September. The Leh–Manali highway and Leh–Srinagar highway are both open during this period. Pangong Lake at 4,350 metres is the most photographed spot, but Nubra Valley, Turtuk village, and the monasteries of Thiksey, Hemis, and Diskit are equally worth your time.
