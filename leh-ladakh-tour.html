About This Package

This six-day circuit covers the essential Ladakh experience without rushing any of it. You land in Leh, spend a day doing very little while your body adjusts to 3,500 metres, and then spend the remaining days crossing some of the highest roads on the planet. Khardungla at 18,380 feet. Chang La at 17,585 feet. Pangong Lake at 14,270 feet. The numbers are impressive but they do not prepare you for what the places actually look like.

Nubra Valley, on the far side of Khardungla, is a cold desert with sand dunes that somehow ended up between mountains. Pangong Tso stretches 134 kilometres into China and the colour of the water does not look real. The monasteries at Thiksey and Shey sit on the hillsides above the Indus as they have for several centuries. Ladakh is one of those places that gets better the more time you spend in it, and this package gives you enough time to actually see it.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1
Arrival in Leh — Acclimatisation

You land at Leh Airport and are met by your driver. The ride to the hotel is short. The rest of the day is free, and this is deliberate. At 3,500 metres the air is genuinely thin and the body needs 24 hours to adjust before anything strenuous. Drink water constantly, eat light, avoid exertion, and sleep early. Everyone who ignores this advice regrets it by day three.

Day 2
Leh — Shyam Valley Full Day Sightseeing

A full day covering the landmarks along the old Srinagar-Leh highway corridor. The Hall of Fame museum near the airport is a thorough and moving record of the Indian Army's campaigns in Ladakh, with photographs, weapons, and personal accounts from soldiers who served here. Gurudwara Pathar Sahib sits on the hillside, peaceful and well-maintained, built around a rock said to bear the imprint of Guru Nanak.

Magnetic Hill is next, where the optical illusion makes cars appear to roll uphill under their own power. The road then climbs to Sangam, the confluence of the Indus and Zanskar rivers. The two rivers meet here in a visible line, the olive-green Indus alongside the grey-white Zanskar, and the contrast between them holds for several hundred metres before the waters mix. Back to Leh Market in the afternoon and up to Shanti Stupa at dusk for panoramic views over the valley.

Day 3
Leh → Khardungla Pass → Nubra Valley → Hunder

Early morning start for the crossing of Khardungla at 18,380 feet. The summit is cold at any time of year, the air is noticeably thinner even compared to Leh, and the view in every direction is the kind of view that makes you understand why people cross high mountain passes at all. The descent into Nubra begins immediately and the landscape changes fast. By the time you reach the valley floor you are in green, irrigated farmland with the Shyok River running alongside.

Diskit Monastery is worth an hour. It sits on a spur of rock above the valley and has been here in some form since the 14th century. The 32-metre Maitreya Buddha statue below it is visible from the road and faces north, watching over the valley. Continue to Hunder village, where the cold desert geology has produced something improbable: sand dunes sitting in the valley between the mountains. Bactrian double-humped camels graze on them in the evening. Overnight in Hunder.

Day 4
Hunder → Pangong Lake via Shyok River

The drive from Hunder to Pangong along the Shyok River is about five hours. The road follows the river for much of the way through a valley that is wide, austere, and very beautiful. The mountains are red and brown with occasional white patches. There are almost no settlements. The road is unpaved in sections but well-travelled.

Pangong Tso arrives without warning. You round a corner and the lake is simply there, enormous and impossibly blue, stretching away into the mountains that mark the Chinese border. At 4,350 metres it is the highest saltwater lake in the world. The colour shifts throughout the day, from deep blue in the morning to turquoise and then greenish in the afternoon. Photographs of Pangong are everywhere in India but they do not fully prepare you for the actual scale of it. Camp overnight at the lakeside.

Day 5
Pangong → Leh via Chang La Pass

Set an alarm for the morning light on Pangong. It is worth it. The lake at 6am, before the other camps wake up, is a different place from the lake at midday. Breakfast at the camp and then the drive back toward Leh via Chang La at 17,585 feet, the third highest motorable road in the world.

The route back passes through some of the best monastery country in Ladakh. Thiksey Monastery is built on a stepped hill above the Indus Valley and is one of the most photographed in the entire region. Shey Palace and the monastery at its foot are on the same road, with a 7.5-metre seated Buddha inside that dates to the 17th century. The 3 Idiots filming location at Rancho's School is nearby for those who want to find it. Sindhu Darshan Ghat at the Indus River is a calm final stop before Leh. Overnight in Leh.

Day 6
Departure from Leh

Early morning transfer to Leh Airport for your onward journey. Six days in Ladakh tends to leave most people wanting to come back. There is always more to see.
